1O BOOKS TO READ TO DEVLOP INTREST IN BOOK READING!!

-BY KHUSHI KUMARI

1. A GOOD GIRL'S GUIDE TO MURDER

The Good Girl's Guide to Murder" is a gripping young adult mystery thriller penned by debut author Holly Jackson. Released in 2019, the novel revolves around Pippa Fitz-Amobi, a high school student who takes on the challenging task of investigating a local murder for her senior project. The story unfolds as Pippa questions the official narrative of Andie Bell's murder, exploring secrets, unraveling lies, and navigating the complexities of the case that led to the conviction of Sal Singh. Jackson expertly blends suspense and a coming-of-age narrative, creating a compelling and engaging story for readers.

In this genre-blending tale, Jackson seamlessly combines mystery, thriller, and contemporary fiction elements. As a young adult novel, it explores the intricacies of high school life while weaving a captivating narrative around Pippa's quest for the truth in a supposedly solved murder case. Holly Jackson's debut work showcases her storytelling prowess, making "The Good Girl's Guide to Murder" an enthralling read for those who enjoy suspenseful mysteries with a youthful perspective.

2. HARRY PORTER

The Harry Potter book series, written by J.K. Rowling, is a globally acclaimed fantasy saga that consists of seven books. The series follows the life and adventures of a young wizard, Harry Potter, and his friends Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, as they attend Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Rowling masterfully weaves a tale of magic, friendship, and the battle between good and evil. As Harry discovers his own magical abilities and unravels the mysteries surrounding his past, he becomes entangled in a battle against the dark wizard Lord Voldemort. The series explores themes of friendship, courage, and the power of love, captivating readers of all ages.

J.K. Rowling's Harry Potter series is a prominent example of fantasy literature, captivating readers with its imaginative world-building and well-developed characters. The seven books in the series, beginning with "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" (titled "Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone" in the U.S.), progress through Harry's journey from a young wizard to a hero facing the challenges of the wizarding world. The books collectively fall under the fantasy genre, enchanting readers with magic, mythical creatures, and a richly detailed magical society.

3. THE GIRL IN ROOM NO. 105

Girl in Room 105" is a suspenseful romantic thriller written by Chetan Bhagat. Published in 2018, the novel blends elements of romance and mystery. The story follows the protagonist, Keshav Rajpurohit, a former student of the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), as he becomes entangled in a mystery involving his ex-girlfriend, Zara Lone, who mysteriously disappears from her hostel room. Keshav embarks on a quest to unravel the truth behind Zara's disappearance, delving into a web of secrets and suspense that takes unexpected twists and turns. Bhagat's narrative not only explores the complexities of modern relationships but also keeps readers on the edge of their seats with its intriguing plot.

Chetan Bhagat's "Girl in Room 105" falls within the genre of romantic thriller, where love and suspense intersect in a gripping narrative. As a bestselling Indian author known for his contemporary fiction, Bhagat weaves a tale that combines elements of romance and mystery, appealing to readers who enjoy a blend of suspense and emotional drama. The novel is marked by its engaging storytelling and relatable characters, making it a compelling read for those who appreciate stories that delve into the intricacies of relationships against a backdrop of suspenseful intrigue.

4. HALF GIRLFRIEND

Half Girlfriend" is a contemporary romance novel written by Indian author Chetan Bhagat. The story revolves around Madhav Jha, a young man from Bihar, who aspires to improve his English language skills and secure a place at Delhi's prestigious St. Stephen's College. In his pursuit of education, he meets Riya Somani, a wealthy and sophisticated girl. As their paths intertwine, a complex relationship develops between them. The term "Half Girlfriend" is coined by Madhav to describe a connection that goes beyond friendship but falls short of a committed relationship. The novel explores themes of love, aspirations, and societal expectations, offering a blend of romance and social commentary.

Chetan Bhagat's "Half Girlfriend" is a contemporary romance that delves into the complexities of modern relationships. Set against the backdrop of Indian society, the novel portrays the challenges faced by its characters as they navigate love, language barriers, and societal expectations. Bhagat's storytelling combines elements of romance, drama, and social commentary, making "Half Girlfriend" a compelling read for those interested in stories that reflect the intricacies of relationships in a changing cultural landscape
